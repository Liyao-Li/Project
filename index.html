Enabling a Continuous, Battery-Free, and Lightweight Wearable Health Monitoring Platform through Advanced Material and Antenna Co-Design

Liyao Li, Yaxiong Xie,
University at Buffalo, SUNY

Abstract

Effective health monitoring is essential for managing chronic diseases, yet current solutions face challenges such as infrastructure dependence, data gaps, and poor user experience. We introduce a low-cost, low-power wearable sensing platform that leverages advanced materials to enable continuous, near-clinical health tracking in everyday environments.

Our work consists of three key innovations:

  • We miniaturize the antenna and embed it into existing wearables like contact lenses to enable long-range backscatter communication (see SmartLens).
  • We enhance the antenna’s functionality by integrating sensing capabilities directly into its structure using advanced materials, without compromising communication performance (see Cyclops).
  • We unify these capabilities into a versatile, plug-and-play platform for multi-modal, wireless, and battery-free wearable sensing (see OhmScanner).

SmartLens: A Passive, Low-cost and Battery-free Eye Movement Sensing System

Liyao Li, Yaxiong Xie, Jie Xiong, Ziyu Hou, Yingchun Zhang, Qing Wei, Fuwei Wang, Dingyi Fang, Xiaojiang Chen
MobiCom 2022
Paper Code Slides BibTeX

Motivation

Eye movements offer a powerful window into both cognitive function and neurological health. As the human eye accounts for approximately 80% of external information acquisition, its activity has been widely adopted as a biomarker for diagnosing conditions such as ADHD, autism, Parkinson’s disease, Alzheimer’s disease, and depression. In addition, eye motion and blinking are key enablers for hands-free human-computer interaction (HCI), especially for individuals with motor impairments.

Despite their promise, existing eye-tracking technologies face critical limitations. Contact-based solutions like electrooculography (EOG) require electrodes to be attached to the skin, which is intrusive and uncomfortable for long-term use. Camera-based systems raise privacy concerns and often suffer from poor robustness under varying lighting conditions. Recent attempts to integrate magnetic or RFID sensing into smart contact lenses have demonstrated limited communication ranges, often restricted to just a few centimeters, which hinders practical deployment in real-world scenarios.

SmartLens miniaturizes a backscatter antenna and integrates it into contact lenses, enabling long-range, battery-free wireless communication for eye-related health monitoring.

To address these challenges, we propose SmartLens—a zero-power, battery-free contact lens system that enables long-range wireless eye activity sensing via UHF RFID backscatter.SmartLens miniaturizes a backscatter antenna and integrates it into contact lenses, enabling long-range, battery-free wireless communication for eye-related health monitoring. It operates reliably at distances over 1.5 meters, supports mobility, and introduces a novel reference tag design to mitigate head movement interference, enabling robust and privacy-preserving eye activity monitoring in a compact, wearable form factor.

Cyclops: A Low-cost, Battery-free, and Long-range Eye Pressure Sensing System

Liyao Li , Bozhao Shang, Yun Wu, Jie Xiong, Xiaojiang Chen, Yaxiong Xie
NSDI 2024
Paper Code Slides BibTeX

Motivation

Intraocular pressure (IOP) is a vital biomarker for eye health, especially for diagnosing and managing glaucoma, the second leading cause of irreversible blindness worldwide. Early detection of elevated IOP is critical for preventing optic nerve damage and vision loss. Continuous, long-range IOP monitoring is essential because IOP naturally fluctuates due to physiological and emotional factors. While contact lens–based sensors are promising due to their comfort and accuracy, existing solutions rely on bulky, short-range readers or batteries, severely limiting practicality.

Cyclops addresses these challenges by enabling continuous, battery-free IOP monitoring within a standard contact lens form factor. It achieves meter-level wireless communication via backscatter and features a novel dual-purpose antenna that simultaneously enables sensing and communication. We functionalize the miniaturized antenna by incorporating sensing capabilities directly into its structure using advanced materials, while preserving communication functionality. This design allows Cyclops to overcome prior range limitations while maintaining high pressure sensitivity, transparency, and biocompatibility—making it a robust platform for real-world deployment.

OhmScanner: A Paradigm shift from signal-based sensing to impedance-based sensing

Liyao Li, Yun Wu, Minsung Kim, Bozhao Shang, Jie Xiong, Wenyao Xu, Xiaojiang Chen, Yaxiong Xie
MobiCom 2025
Paper Code Slides BibTeX

Motivation

Battery-free sensing has emerged as a transformative paradigm for Internet of Things (IoT) applications, enabling sustainable, low-cost, and continuous monitoring across domains such as human-object interaction, environmental sensing, and food safety. However, existing solutions rely heavily on signal variations in the wireless communication channel, making them highly susceptible to environmental dynamics and deployment variations. These vulnerabilities prevent robust, plug-and-play sensing in practical scenarios and limit the scalability of current approaches.

To overcome these challenges, OhmScanner introduces a fundamental shift from signal-based to impedance-based sensing. By directly measuring variations in antenna impedance—rather than relying on unstable backscatter signals—OhmScanner decouples the sensing process from the wireless channel. This enables reliable, deployment-independent sensing, even in highly dynamic and mobile environments.

Furthermore, we generalize these innovations into a versatile platform that supports multi-modal, plug-and-play wireless battery-free wearable sensing, demonstrating its robustness across diverse applications including finger tracking, button interfaces, and liquid identification. Through advanced antenna design and material integration, OhmScanner significantly expands the frontier of what battery-free sensing can achieve.
